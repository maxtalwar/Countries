Fun Facts:

  • Highest life expectancy: 83.6 Years
  • Japan’s population is 98.5% Japanese
  • Each Year There Are Over 1,500 Earthquakes
  • Tokyo is the World’s Most Populated City: 38.14 million

Demographics:

Okinawa

What to do: Swimming, diving, and snorkeling along its colorful coral reefs

Fun Fact: Okinawa was the site of the last big battle of World War II, the Battle of Okinawa

Hokkaidō

What to do: Visit the Hakodate Morning Market, enjoy the views from the top of Mount Hakodate, and see Goryokaku Fort

Fun Fact: It's Japan's biggest island (22% of total land)

Tōhoku

What to do: Explore the beautiful Ginzan Onsen, ski and bathe at Mount Zao, and vist Aizu Temple for its samurai history

Fun Fact: Experience a magnitude 9.0 earthquake (most powerful in Japan since 1900)

Chūbu

What to do: Admire the beauty of the Shirakawago village, climb mount Fuji, and ski at Hakuba

Fun Fact: Mount Fuji is in Chūbu

Kansai

What to do: Tour the famous Kiyomizu-dera temple, participate in the Osaka night life, and eat Kobe beef in Kobe

Fun Fact: Kyoto use to be capital of Japan

Kantō

What to do: Yike Takao mountain, ride Fuji-q Highland's record breaking rollercoasters, and walk around Tokyo at night

Fun Fact: Tokyo has a higher population than the entire country of Canada

Chūgoku

What to do: Visit the Hiroshima memorial, see the floating torii gate at Miyajima, and eat fugu(pufferfish) Shimonoseki

Fun Fact: Oldest surviving castles and towns can be found here

Shikoku

What to do: View the Naruto whirlpools, experience the Tokushima dance festival, and travel around the Seto Inland Sea to see all the marine life and nature

Fun Fact: Smallest of Japan's 4 islands

Kyūshū

What to do: Go to Fukuoka to eat the original tonkotsu ramen, take in the beautiful Aso mountain, and relax in the onsens at Beppu

Fun Fact: Aso mountain is an active volcano
